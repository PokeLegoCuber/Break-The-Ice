Knowing your constitutional rights are very important and can save you from a terrible encounter, The 5th amendment is the "Rights of the accused - a person accused of a crime is entitled to certain rights". The 14th amendment is granted citizenship to all persons born or naturalized in the United States—including former slaves and guaranteed all citizens “equal protection of the laws.” The 6th amendment "Right to a speedy trial by jury and a lawyer". There are so many resources that can get you a free lawyer if needed.The New York State Office for New Americans has a phone number to help and connect immigrants to legal advice that is hosted on weekends: 1-800-566-7636. "The New York Immigrant Family Unity Project (NYIFUP) is funded by the city and its a program that provides free immigration legal representation to all immigrants who have been detained/ caught by ICE and have their hearings in New York City. What is DACA?

The short story is that DACA has made a way for children and young adults who came to the United States with their parents to legally obtain a Social Security Number, driver’s license, enroll in college and work.

Considerations for LGBTQ Immigrants

LGBTQ immigrants have the same rights under most immigration laws as all other non-citizens. However there might be some challenges.

Red Card

All people in the United States, regardless of immigration status, have certain rights and protections under the U.S. Constitution (amedments like 5th,6th,and 14th). The Red Cards were created to help people get their rights and be able to defend themselves against violations. Knowing this information can help make rights that can make a huge difference in many situations, such as when ICE agents go to a home.
